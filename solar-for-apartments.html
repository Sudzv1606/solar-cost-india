Can apartment residents benefit from solar?

The answer depends on your state policy, society rules, and rooftop availability.

✅ Policy data verified: March 2025

Why is it different for flats?

Unlike independent houses, you share the roof and the rules.

🏢

One Roof, Many Meters

Physically, the roof belongs to everyone. Connecting one plant to 100+ individual meters requires specific "Virtual Metering" technology.

📍

State Policy is Key

Some states (like Maharashtra & Delhi) allow "shared benefits". Others only allow solar for common area lights & lifts.

🤝

Society Consent

You cannot install typical private solar without RWA Permission. It's a community project, not a personal one.

Talking to your society about solar

Most RWAs reject solar because they fear damage to the roof or complex billing. Here is how to start a productive conversation:

1. Start with "Common Areas"

Don't ask for personal solar first. Propose solar for Lifts, Pumps, and Corridor Lighting. This creates immediate savings on the Maintenance Bill, benefiting everyone (even ground floor residents).

2. Address "Roof Rights"

Clarify that the solar plant is a temporary asset. It does not transfer ownership of the roof. The terrace remains common property.

3. Check for "Group Metering"

If you are in a supported state (like Delhi/Maharashtra), bring the official policy circular to the meeting. Show that it is legal and standard.
