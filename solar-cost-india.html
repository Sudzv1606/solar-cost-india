What Affects Solar Cost in India

Understanding why prices vary - the factors that impact your final cost

1. System Size (kW)

Economies of scale apply:

  • 1-2 kW: Highest per kW cost
  • 3-5 kW: Optimal price point
  • 6-10 kW: Lowest per kW cost
  • Above 10 kW: Commercial rates apply

Example: A 3 kW system might cost ₹1.8L total (₹60k/kW), while a 1 kW system costs ₹70k (₹70k/kW)

2. Roof Type & Strength

Installation complexity matters:

  • Flat concrete roof: Standard cost
  • Slanted/tiled roof: 10-15% extra
  • Sheet metal roof: 5-10% extra
  • High-rise building: 20-30% extra
  • Structural reinforcement: Additional cost if needed

Example: Same 3 kW system costs ₹60k/kW on ground floor, ₹78k/kW on 10th floor

3. Location & State Policies

Regional variations:

  • Transport distance from ports
  • Local labor rates
  • State taxes and duties
  • Installer competition
  • DISCOM efficiency

Example: A 3 kW system in Maharashtra often costs less than the same system in Kerala due to installer density and approval timelines

4. Inverter & Panel Quality

Technology choices impact cost:

  • String inverter: Most economical
  • Micro inverters: 25-30% extra
  • Poly panels: Standard efficiency
  • Mono PERC panels: 10-15% extra
  • Premium brands: 20-25% premium

Example: Same size system with premium panels and micro inverters can cost 30% more

5. Net Metering Rules

State DISCOM policies:

  • Application fee: ₹500 - ₹5,000
  • Net meter cost: Varies (often free)
  • Approval timeline: 30-90 days
  • Carry forward rules: 1 month to 1 year

Example: Delhi has instant net metering approval, some states take 3+ months

MNRE Subsidy Explained

Understanding central government support - who gets it and how much

PM-Surya Ghar Yojana (2025)

₹78,000

For systems up to 2 kW

Additional for 2-3 kW

₹39,000

Total ₹1,17,000 for 3 kW systems

Above 3 kW

₹1,17,000

Capped at 3 kW equivalent

Who May Be Eligible:

  • Residential property owners
  • Grid-connected homes (no battery)
  • Using MNRE-approved components
  • Installed by MNRE-empanelled installer

Why Many Don't Get Full Subsidy:

  • Apartments often ineligible
  • Subsidy capped at 3 kW
  • State subsidies vary (some have none)
  • Documentation requirements strict
  • Approval timeline can be long

Central vs State Roles:

Central (MNRE): Provides the ₹78,000-₹1,17,000 subsidy

State Governments: May add extra subsidy or facilitate faster processing

DISCOMs: Handle net metering and technical approvals

Important: Never assume you will get subsidy. Always check eligibility and understand the application process before committing.

Is Solar Worth It in India?

Understanding when solar makes financial sense - this is framing, not specific calculations

Typical Payback: 4-7 Years

3-4 Years

High tariff states (Delhi, Maharashtra) + Good sun

4-5 Years

Most states with ₹5,000+ monthly bills

5-7 Years

Lower tariff states but still viable

7+ Years

Low consumption or expensive locations

✅ Solar Usually Makes Sense

  • Monthly electricity bill above ₹3,000
  • Own your house with shadow-free roof
  • Planning to stay 5+ years
  • State has good net metering policy
  • Area has frequent power cuts

Reason: High consumption + high tariffs = faster payback

❌ Solar Often Does NOT Make Sense

  • Monthly bill below ₹1,500
  • Living in rented apartment
  • Heavy roof shadows
  • Planning to move in 2-3 years
  • No access to roof (society terrace)

Reason: Low savings can't justify investment
