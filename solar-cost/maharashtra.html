Local rules to be aware of in Maharashtra

Processing Fees: MSEDCL typically charges a processing fee (approx. ₹500-₹2000) for net metering applications, which is often excluded from installer quotes.
Sanctioned Load Check: Solar capacity cannot exceed your sanctioned load. Upgrading your load may move you to a higher fixed-charge slab.
* These are procedural considerations, not deal-breakers.

Solar Installation Cost in Maharashtra

Updated for 2025: Subsidy rules, MSEDCL tariffs, and official price trends.

✅ Last reviewed: March 2025

Rooftop solar costs and savings in Maharashtra vary from the national average due to electricity tariffs, approval timelines, and installer density. This page explains how residential solar pricing works specifically in Maharashtra and what homeowners should expect before installing solar.

How solar costs in Maharashtra compare to India

Cost Range

₹50,000 - ₹75,000 per kW

Slightly below national average in most cities, excluding Mumbai where costs are higher

Electricity Provider

MSEDCL - Maharashtra State Electricity Distribution Co. Ltd.

Approval Timeline

30-45 days

Faster in urban areas, rural may take longer

In Maharashtra, residential rooftop solar installation costs are typically lower than the national average in many cities, especially outside Mumbai. This is largely due to:

  • A mature installer ecosystem
  • Standardized DISCOM processes
  • Widespread net metering availability

However, prices can still vary depending on city, roof type, system size, and inverter and panel quality. For exact estimates, results should always be refined using a calculator with location selected.

Electricity tariffs & impact on savings

Residential electricity tariffs in Maharashtra are moderate to high compared to several other states. This means:

  • Homes with higher monthly bills typically see faster payback
  • Solar becomes more viable above a certain bill threshold
  • Savings depend on how much of your consumption is offset by solar

Tariffs and billing slabs are defined by the state DISCOM and can differ slightly across regions.

Typical payback period in Maharashtra

4-6 years for properly sized systems, depending on location and consumption patterns

Subsidy & approvals in Maharashtra

MNRE subsidy

Central MNRE subsidy applies to eligible residential rooftop systems

Subsidy availability depends on system size, roof ownership, and grid connection

Final approval is subject to documentation and DISCOM verification

DISCOM process

In Maharashtra, approvals and net metering are handled primarily by MSEDCL (Mahavitaran).

Typical approval timelines range from:

  • Application → installation: a few weeks
  • Net meter activation: may take additional time

Delays usually occur due to incomplete documentation, site inspection backlogs, or meter availability.

Is solar worth it in Maharashtra?

Solar is often financially viable when:

  • Monthly electricity bills are moderate to high
  • The homeowner owns the roof
  • The system is sized appropriately

Solar may be less suitable when:

  • Roof access is limited
  • The home is rented
  • Electricity usage is very low
