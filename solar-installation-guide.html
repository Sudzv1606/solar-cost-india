Phase 1: Decision & Feasibility

Evaluating whether solar makes sense for your home and situation

🔍 Roof Inspection

✓
Shadow Analysis:

Check for shadows from trees, buildings, water tanks. Solar needs 5+ hours of direct sun.

✓
Available Space:

Minimum 300 sq ft shadow-free area needed for 3 kW system.

✓
Roof Condition:

Ensure roof can support 10-15 kg/sqm. Check for water seepage or structural issues.

✓
Orientation:

South-facing ideal. East-West acceptable. North-facing not suitable.

⚠️ Load Assessment

Review Your Electricity Bills:

  • Collect last 12 months of bills
  • Identify peak consumption months (summer)
  • Check sanctioned load vs actual consumption
  • Determine optimal system size
Quick Rule: 1 kW ≈ ₹800-1,000 monthly savings. Match system size to your highest bill month.

🚩 Common Red Flags

Monthly bill below ₹1,500:

Payback period exceeds 7-8 years. Not economically viable.

Heavy shadows throughout day:

Generation reduced by 30-50%. Solar won't be cost-effective.

Rented apartment:

Not eligible for subsidy. Can't recover investment.

Planning to move in 2-3 years:

Payback period longer than your stay.

Very old roof (>15 years):

May need reinforcement, increasing costs significantly.

Phase 2: Application & Approvals

Navigating the bureaucratic process - MNRE portal, DISCOM approvals, and documentation

📍 ‘ MNRE Portal Registration

Step 1: Create Account

Register on national solar portal with Aadhaar and PAN

Step 2: Upload Documents

Latest electricity bill, property papers, bank details

Step 3: Select Installer

Choose MNRE-empanelled installer (only for subsidy)

Step 4: Technical Details

System specification, single line diagram

🏛️ DISCOM Application

Required Documents:

✓ Latest electricity bill (original)
✓ Property tax receipt
✓ Ownership proof
✓ Aadhaar card
✓ PAN card
✓ Bank details
✓ Rooftop photos
✓ Single line diagram

Application Process:

  1. Submission: Online or at DISCOM office
  2. Fee Payment: ₹500-5,000 (varies by state)
  3. Application Number: Get acknowledgment
  4. Tracking: Reference number for status updates

⏱️ Approval Timelines

Fast States (30-45 days)

Gujarat, Delhi, Maharashtra (major cities)

Online portals, efficient processing

Average States (45-60 days)

Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Telangana

Moderate workload, semi-online

Slow States (60-90+ days)

UP, Bihar, Northeast states

Manual processing, high backlog

What You Get:

  • Letter of Approval (LOA): Valid for 6 months
  • Technical Feasibility Report: System approval
  • Net Metering Approval: Connection permission
  • Installation Guidelines: Technical specifications

Phase 3: Installation

The physical installation process - typically 3-7 days for residential systems

🛠️ Installation Steps

Day 1: Mounting Structure

  • Site preparation and cleaning
  • Mark drilling points
  • Install mounting frames
  • Waterproofing all penetrations

Most critical day - determines long-term performance

Day 2: Panel Installation

  • Mount solar panels on structure
  • Connect panels in series/parallel
  • Route DC cables safely
  • Secure all connections

Day 3: Inverter & Electrical

  • Install inverter near meter
  • Setup ACDB/DCDB
  • Connect to existing wiring
  • Earthing and lightning protection

Requires certified electrician

Days 4-5: Testing & Commissioning

  • Continuity and insulation tests
  • Inverter parameter setup
  • Initial grid synchronization
  • Performance verification

📍 Quality Checklist

Structural Safety

  • All mounting bolts tightened to specified torque
  • Proper sealant on all roof penetrations
  • Adequate wind load calculations done
  • Weight distributed evenly

Electrical Safety

  • MC4 connectors properly locked
  • Cable trays and conduits used
  • Proper earthing (2 pits minimum)
  • Lightning arrestor installed

System Performance

  • Panel tilt angle optimal for location
  • No inter-panel shading
  • Inverter settings configured correctly
  • Generation monitoring enabled

💡 Pro Tip:

Take photos of each installation stage. Create a time-lapse if possible. This helps with warranty claims and resale value.

Phase 4: Commissioning

The final phase where your system goes live and starts generating savings

🔌 DISCOM Inspection

What They Check:

  • Compliance: Matches approved specifications?
  • Safety: Proper earthing, lightning protection?
  • Quality: MNRE-approved components used?
  • Connections: Proper wiring standards followed?
  • Location: Installation matches application?

Possible Outcomes:

✓
Approved:

Everything compliant, proceed to net meter

⚠️
Minor Issues:

Small corrections needed, re-inspection in 3-5 days

✗
Major Issues:

Significant rework required, 2-4 weeks delay

🌐 Net Meter Installation

What Happens:

  1. Application: Submit inspection report and test certificates
  2. Scheduling: DISCOM team visits within 10-20 days
  3. Installation: Bi-directional meter replaces old meter
  4. Sealing: Meter sealed to prevent tampering
  5. Configuration: Net metering settings programmed

Types of Net Meters:

  • Import-Export (Single Element): Most common, measures both
  • Bi-directional (Two Elements): Separate import and export
  • Smart Meters: Advanced, with remote reading capability

🚀 System Goes Live

Final Activation:

  • System synchronized with grid
  • All safety interlocks tested
  • Monitoring app configured
  • First generation reading recorded

First Week Expectations:

System will generate 4-6 kWh per kW per day. Monitor daily generation. It may vary due to weather, but should stabilize within 7 days.

Quick Performance Check:

Rule of thumb: 1 kW ≈ 4-5 units per day average. If getting less than 50% consistently, contact installer.

Post-Commissioning:

  • Subsidy Application: Submit final installation report
  • Warranty Registration: Register panels and inverter
  • Maintenance Schedule: Set up cleaning and service plan
  • Monitoring Setup: Configure app alerts

Common Problems & How to Avoid Them

Understanding and preventing typical installation issues

⏰ Delayed Approvals

Cause: Incomplete documents, manual processing, high volume

Prevention:
  • Double-check all documents before submission
  • Apply during off-peak season (Oct-March)
  • Use online portals where available
  • Follow up regularly with reference number

⚡ Low Generation

Cause: Shadows, wrong orientation, faulty panels, inverter issues

Prevention:
  • Professional shadow analysis before installation
  • Verify panel specifications and performance
  • Ensure proper inverter configuration
  • Regular cleaning and maintenance

📍 Warranty Confusion

Cause: Not understanding different warranties, unclear terms

Prevention:
  • Panel warranty: 25 years performance degradation
  • Inverter warranty: 5-10 years comprehensive
  • Workmanship warranty: 1-3 years
  • AMC terms clearly documented

💰 Hidden Costs

Cause: Structural reinforcement, extra civil work, permits

Prevention:
  • Structural assessment before finalizing quote
  • Clear all-inclusive pricing in contract
  • DISCOM fee structure understood upfront
  • Contingency fund (10% of total)

🏠 Roof Leaks

Cause: Improper waterproofing of mounting points

Prevention:
  • Use proper sealant on all penetrations
  • Install mounting flashings
  • Water test after installation
  • Monitor during first monsoon

📊 Subsidy Delays

Cause: Documentation errors, MNRE backlog, state bureaucracy

Prevention:
  • Engage MNRE-empanelled installer
  • Complete documentation package
  • Apply immediately after installation
  • Follow up with installer and portal
